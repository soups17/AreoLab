ABOUT THE PHYSICS MODEL

Section aerodynamics come from a Hess–Smith source/vortex panel method solved live on the actual contour — ~112 panels, flow tangency plus a Kutta condition at the trailing edge. Any shape you draw or import gets real potential-flow pressures, lift slope, zero-lift angle, and quarter-chord moment. The full α-range is a superposition of two basis solutions, so nothing re-solves as you sweep.

3D wing effects apply a lifting-line style correction (aspect ratio, Oswald efficiency, sweep) to the panel-method section, with pressures unloaded elliptically toward the tips.

Stall is empirical: potential flow is inviscid, so the attached solution blends into a flat-plate curve near an estimated stall angle and the suction side develops a separation plateau.

Honest scope: panel method + lifting line + empirical viscous effects — real engineering methods, but not RANS/LES CFD. Profile drag is estimated, not resolved.

The section is discretized into 112 panels, each carrying a constant source strength plus one shared vortex strength. Flow tangency at every panel midpoint and a Kutta condition at the trailing edge give a 113×113 linear system, solved for two freestream directions — every angle of attack below is a superposition of those two solutions.
