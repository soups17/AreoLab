ABOUT THE PHYSICS MODEL

Section aerodynamics come from a Hess–Smith source/vortex panel method solved live on the actual contour — ~112 panels, flow tangency plus a Kutta condition at the trailing edge. Any shape you draw or import gets real potential-flow pressures, lift slope, zero-lift angle, and quarter-chord moment. The full α-range is a superposition of two basis solutions, so nothing re-solves as you sweep.

3D wing effects apply a lifting-line style correction (aspect ratio, Oswald efficiency, sweep) to the panel-method section, with pressures unloaded elliptically toward the tips.

Stall is empirical: potential flow is inviscid, so the attached solution blends into a flat-plate curve near an estimated stall angle and the suction side develops a separation plateau.

Honest scope: panel method + lifting line + empirical viscous effects — real engineering methods, but not RANS/LES CFD. Profile drag is estimated, not resolved.

AEROLAB
INTRODUCTION — 1 OF 5

Welcome. AeroLab is an interactive aerodynamic analysis environment that runs entirely in your browser. Section aerodynamics are solved live by a Hess–Smith vortex panel method on the exact airfoil contour; a lifting-line correction extends the solution to a full three-dimensional wing. Nothing here is pre-rendered — every number and every frame is computed as you work.

This brief tour covers the controls, how to read the results, and where the deeper tools live. It takes about a minute.

The tunnel. The left panel defines the aircraft: airfoil shape (camber, thickness, four NACA presets), the 3D planform (chord, span, taper, sweep, dihedral), and the flow condition (angle of attack, airspeed). The viewport responds instantly — drag to orbit, scroll to zoom, and use RESET VIEW in the header to reframe.

Enable Auto-sweep α in the display panel to fly the wing through its full envelope, including the stall, hands-free.

Reading the physics. The smoke is a language: platinum air has not met the wing; air that passes over it turns teal permanently; a separating, turbulent wake shifts coral; contact with the surface glows white; and the rotating helices behind each wingtip are the tip vortices — the physical cost of finite span.

The right panel carries live coefficients and forces, the stall status pill, and three charts: surface pressure distribution, the lift curve with the stall marked, and the drag polar.

Bring your own geometry. ✎ DESIGN CUSTOM opens a control-point editor with a live panel solution as you drag. LOAD .DAT accepts standard coordinate files — any airfoil in the UIUC database. The CAD PART group imports an STL from Fusion 360, Creo, or AutoCAD and slices a wing section directly into the solver. SAVE .DAT exports your shape back out.

Beyond the tunnel. Calcs opens the live worksheet — every equation behind these numbers with your values substituted, plus a full-page version with editable atmosphere and flight performance. Design Studio sizes a complete aircraft around this wing — weight build-up, motor–prop–battery matching, envelope, CG, and tail — right here in the lab, and APPLY WING TO TUNNEL pushes its wing straight back into the tunnel. The URL always encodes your exact configuration — copy it to share this precise state.

▮ 3 · SECTION — HESS–SMITH PANEL METHOD

The section is discretized into 112 panels, each carrying a constant source strength plus one shared vortex strength. Flow tangency at every panel midpoint and a Kutta condition at the trailing edge give a 113×113 linear system, solved for two freestream directions — every angle of attack below is a superposition of those two solutions.
